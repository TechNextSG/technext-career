📋 Scenario

GreenTech Malaysia — B2B SaaS Go-to-Market

GreenTech Malaysia is a B2B sustainability consulting firm launching a new carbon accounting SaaS platform. They have a RM 200,000 marketing budget and a 6-month go-to-market window. Their target audience is CFOs and sustainability directors at listed companies. They have no existing digital presence and minimal brand recognition in the sector.

MCQ 1 of 2 — Channel Strategy

What should be the primary channel focus for GreenTech's go-to-market launch?

MCQ 2 of 2 — KPI Selection

GreenTech wants to measure success across 6 months. Which set of KPIs is most appropriate for their stage?
✍️ Written Response
GreenTech's marketing director asks you to recommend a content marketing strategy that positions them as thought leaders and generates inbound leads. Outline your approach — including content formats, topics, distribution channels, and a brief rationale for why this strategy fits their audience and go-to-market stage.

💡 Consider: What content proves credibility to CFOs? Where do sustainability directors consume information? How does thought leadership connect to lead generation?

🔥 Crisis Scenario

PulseWear — Viral Brand Crisis Response

PulseWear is a mid-tier wearable tech brand. A viral social media post has accused them of misleading health claims on their flagship fitness tracker. The post has 500,000+ shares and is trending nationally. Media outlets are picking it up. The marketing team must respond within 4 hours.

MCQ 1 of 2 — Immediate Response

What is the most critical first action the marketing team should take?

MCQ 2 of 2 — Trust Rebuilding

After the immediate crisis is addressed, which strategy best rebuilds brand trust over the following months?
✍️ Written Response
You are the senior marketing lead at PulseWear. Draft a crisis communication framework — including the initial public response (key message and tone), stakeholder communication priorities (employees, media, customers), and a 30-60-90 day reputation recovery roadmap.
0 characters
💡 Consider: What tone does the brand need to strike (defensive vs. transparent)? What are the risks of silence vs. early acknowledgement? Who needs to hear from you first?

🤖 AI Strategy Scenario

RetailX — AI Personalisation at Scale

RetailX is a mid-sized e-commerce retailer with 800,000 active customers. They send weekly email newsletters to their entire list with the same content. Email open rate: 12% (industry average: 22%). Click-through rate: 1.1% (industry average: 3.5%). The CMO has allocated budget for AI-driven personalisation but the team lacks implementation experience.

MCQ 1 of 2 — Root Cause Diagnosis

What is the most likely primary cause of RetailX's below-average email performance metrics?

MCQ 2 of 2 — Measuring Success

The team implements AI-powered product recommendation emails. Which outcome best indicates the personalisation strategy is working?
✍️ Written Response
Outline a 90-day AI personalisation implementation roadmap for RetailX's email marketing. Include: the data you would need to collect, the AI tools or capabilities you would implement (and why), how you would measure success, and any risks or ethical considerations the team should be aware of.
0 characters
💡 Consider: What data signals drive personalisation? What are the risks of over-reliance on AI? How do you balance personalisation with customer privacy?
